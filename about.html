To populate this new world, they brought along rabbits, one of Earth's most adaptable creatures. But the Moon held an unexpected secret: its dust had strange and transformative properties.

Over time, the rabbits roaming this lush lunar paradise began to change. Moon dust infected them, endowing them with humanoid bodies and an awakened intelligence.

One day, the humans suddenly disappeared. Leaving no warning, no explanation, they abandoned the Moon, leaving behind the paradise they had created and the rabbits within it. Over the years, the reason for this abandonment became legend and a mystery buried beneath the lush lunar landscape.

Over time, the rabbits became more intelligent and aware, curiously exploring their surroundings. Books, clothes, tools and objects left behind by humans were found scattered across the green plains of the Moon. Fascinated by these objects, the rabbits began to imitate what they found. They learned to read from old and dusty books, transformed the clothes they discovered into simple outfits and even tried to look like their creators by wearing wigs and hats.

Through this imitation, the rabbits, now known as Tuzi, developed their own society, blending their natural instincts with the knowledge and culture they learned from humans. They gathered together to read old human books aloud.
